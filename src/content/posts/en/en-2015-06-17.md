First, the usual signs of a company just before bankruptcy.

**1. The balance sheet in the IR materials shows a lot of debt**

Once it exceeds roughly 80%, it is a danger zone.

**2. The income statement in the IR materials keeps showing losses**

Continuing losses when you look at company profit are a dangerous sign.

**3. Operating cash flow in the IR materials keeps being negative**

Operating cash flow is what you look at to see the numbers of the core business.

**4. There are more meetings about sales**

When meetings increase, danger signs are starting to appear.

**5. The company issues corporate bonds**

Sometimes companies issue private bonds for employees.

**6. Elite talent and administrative departments start leaving**

Elite people and people who manage the numbers, such as accounting, leave first.

**7. It becomes hard to reach the president**

They are often out raising money, so they become hard to contact.

**8. Excessive cost cutting**

Pointless cost-cutting projects are a dangerous sign.

**9. Salary payments start being delayed**

Once salaries start being paid late, it is finally serious.

Next, the programmer's version: how to spot a company headed for bankruptcy.

**1. There is a lot of code without test code**

Once it exceeds roughly 80%, it is a danger zone.

**2. Commit frequency is dropping**

A decline in commits is a dangerous sign.

**3. Meaningless commits keep appearing**

Source code is what you look at to see the numbers of the core business.

**4. There are more meetings about progress**

When meetings increase, danger signs are starting to appear.

**5. Overtime happens**

Sometimes people also work on holidays.

**6. Elite talent and project managers start leaving**

Elite people and people who manage schedules leave first.

**7. It becomes hard to reach the project manager**

They are often busy contacting people about delayed deadlines, so they become hard to reach.

**8. Excessive consecutive workdays**

Pointless consecutive work is a dangerous sign.

**9. Programmers start taking sick leave**

Once programmers start taking sick leave, it is finally serious.
